context

If you've ever applied to colleges in the US (or anywhere else in the world), you probably remember the many hours of college research. College websites, social media, talking to friends and alumni, campus tours... you name it. It can be tiring and overwhelming.

The University Web aims to help high school students decide where they want to go to college by allowing them to get a look and feel of what universities look like on the inside. During Spring 2021, I worked with three other Brown students on this app: Hunter Karas (who came up with the idea), Aanchal Sheth, and William Bednarz.

app breakdown
Our app has two primary users - college students who will post and view content, and high school students who will only view content. According to the email that users sign up with, our app will check whether they are a college student or not. Therefore, college students that want to post content will have to sign up with a college email, along with a password. High school students can sign up with any email they want. If a user is a high school student, they will have the ability to view posts, but not create them.

We decided to go with a UI similar to that of Instagram where users can follow different universities and then scroll through content that college students from those universities post. We also think that an Instagram-like UI will make navigating the app easier since most users are probably familiar with Instagram's layout.
technology used
  • Frontend: React Native
  • Backend: Python and Flask
  • Database: Firebase
page breakdown
Below is a breakdown of each page of the app, with a list of the features on that page.
  • Login + Signup page: username and password authentication
  • Feed page: allows users to scroll through content posted by college students. Also includes:
    • like/unlike functionality, by clicking on heart icon or double tapping an image
    • Report button
    • Navigation to university profile page by clicking on the university's name in a post's header
  • University profile: Aggregates all of the posts that college students from that university have posted and then classifies them into 4 categories: campus life, social life, academics, food. This page includes the following features:
    • Navigation bar that allows user to toggle between categories (campus, social, academics, food)
    • Settings button
  • Student profile: Aggregates all of the posts that a student has posted (if they are a college student) and all of that user's liked posts
    • Navigation bar that allows user to toggle between 2 categories: all posts and liked posts
    • Settings button
  • Create post page: Allows user to create a post, either by taking a photo or uploading a photo from camera roll
    • Accept camera permissions
    • Take photo (user can also flip camera to back mode) or upload photo
    • Navigate to photo editing page where user can write a caption, choose a category, and then post to the feed
design choices + accessibility
  • We decided to create a mobile app as opposed to a web app. We thought this would be more practical since users that post will need to take photos and upload photos.
  • Mimicking popular apps makes it easier for users to navigate our app.
  • In terms of accessibility, we did the following:
    • tested for compatibility on different devices
    • good color contrast
    • optimal button sizes
    • consistency across the app
    • bottom navigation bar allows users to easily "undo" actions
    • captions for images can be read by screen readers for visually impaired users
security + privacy
  • Privacy
    • We decided to make posts anonymous i.e. the post is under the university's name, not the user's. We also thought that this would encourage users to be more honest.
    • User has to agree to our privacy policy when signing up. This is because our app asks for camera permissions and because we plan to launch our app on the App Store, which requires a privacy policy if the app collects user data.
  • Security
    • We decided to use a Firebase database because it is an existing API and is certified under major privacy and security standards.
    • Data Validation:
      • Email and password authentication
      • Checking length of user input to prevent system overload
      • Profanity checker and report button
